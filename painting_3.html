Yueying Zhong's Ink Painting

  •  

Mr. Zhong has been focusing his art creation on exploring the expression of ink painting. He has tried to excavate the various possibilities of ink and rice paper (xuan zhi) and to dig out new artistic elements. When he did a painting, a great amount of water was used as it could provide layers of the ink and express different depths of structure. He has tested the endurance of rice paper, letting ink and water flow freely to create new images and styles. Zhong’s works represent his achievement in all these explorations and have built up a new art language of contemporary ink painting. Indeed, Zhong’s philosophy lies in advocating our “ inner nature”, which means, “following our own spirits and characters”. His landscapes, for example, don’t present what we see but what he had in his inner mind. The language communicated through the abstract expression of ink is called yixiang (mind images) and yijing (mind compositions), referring to the images formed between natural inspiration and his personal insight. Zong’s new large-scaled paintings with summarized and simplified details of description have created a vivid and alive atmosphere (rhythmic vitality), delineating clearly the essence of ink painting: “simple means more”. This has also confirmed what Shih-Tao, a great artist of the early Qing period (17th c.) had said, “One stroke’s principle is for all.”

Meilan Sun, Professor of Art
Central Academy of Fine Arts, China
