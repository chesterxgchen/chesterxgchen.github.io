Yueying Zhong's Ink Painting

  •  

Zhong’s painting communicated a deep sophistication that reflected a subtle, simultaneous balancing of abstraction and reference to the natural world. Dramatic expanses of black-space –created with radical pooling of ink-were often alive with texture suggesting vegetation. But what impressed me most was that these dynamic compositions instantly spoke to me in the complex language of painting about life’s continuity, and change. Zhong’s landscape paintings appear to be mapping the formation of stone, showing the reaction of elements including water earth and wood. These paintings are magnificent works of art that operate both metaphysically and trans-nationally; they walk the transparent cutting edge of knife that divides abstraction from representation, while they simultaneously extend the beautifully silent and moonlit language of Chinese painting from California into the international Twenty-first century.

Mark Dean Johnson, Professor of Art
San Francisco State University, USA
